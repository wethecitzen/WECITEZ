If the Lok Sabha elections have not yet been announced, the movement of political parties across the country has increased. Alliance-led politics has also started. On the same background, Congress President Rahul Gandhi is likely to launch the forthcoming Lok Sabha election from Dhule in Maharashtra. On March 1, Rahul Gandhi's public meeting was held in Dhule, sources said. Congress is preparing to show big power in Dhule assembly. In addition, Priyanka Gandhi's rally in Maharashtra has been organized by Congress next month.

Later, on 20th March, Congress and NCP's joint campaign will be held in Nanded. NCP chief Sharad Pawar, Ashok Chavan, Maharashtra's in-charge Mallikarjun Kharge will also participate in this rally.

Mahagathbandhan in Maharashtra is set to start its campaign from Nanded. The first rally of joint opposition will be on 20th March in Nanded NCP's Sharad Pawar, Congress's Mallikarjun Kharge and Ashok Chavan - ANI (@ANI) February 18, 2019

There are likely to be elections in April-May. The Election Commission has not yet been declared. Rahul Gandhi will start campaigning for Dhule. Sources know that this will be their first meeting of the campaign.

Dhule is suitable for party workers, and activists from Jalgaon, Nandurbar, neighboring districts can participate in this rally, said the activist. Rahul Gandhi has requested the senior functionaries of the state to hold 4 to 5 meetings.

Meanwhile, Maharashtra Pradesh Congress Committee President Ashok Chavan has been asked about this, he is planning to hold a meeting of Rahul Gandhi in Dhule, but he has not yet finalized the decision.
